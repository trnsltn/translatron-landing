Q: Should I use human translators or machine translation?

A: Both. MT for speed and cost (80% done instantly), humans for the 20% that drives conversions (CTAs, headlines, value props). A hybrid approach costs 60-70% less than full human translation while maintaining quality where it matters.

Q: Which languages should I launch with first?

A: Check your data first (Analytics ‚Üí Audience ‚Üí Geo), but generally: Spanish (577M speakers, Americas + Europe), French (280M speakers, high purchasing power), or German (economically powerful market). Don't guess‚Äîfollow the traffic.

Q: What if I need to update translations frequently?

A: This is exactly why platforms with translation memory save money. You only pay for new/changed strings. Updates cost 70-80% less than initial translations.

Q: How do I handle plurals and gender in different languages?

A: Use ICU MessageFormat or similar (most modern frameworks support this). Your localization platform should handle this automatically‚Äîif it doesn't, it's not built for developers.

Q: Can I really do this with a small team?

A: Yes. Solo founders are shipping multilingual apps. With automation, localization takes 1-2 hours per month, not 20+. The key is choosing tools that eliminate manual work.
